Leonardo da Vinci's very accurate map of Imola

Very accurate indeed, and very beautiful I would add. I love old maps and their power in revealing the changes of the land over time, but I must admit, I never heard of this one untill very recently.

We have a book on Leonardo Da Vinci and my five year old son was looking at some drawings on it. And because I saw him courious, I searched the Web for more and there, on a wikipedia page, I found it.

"Leonardo created a map of Cesare Borgia's stronghold, a town plan of Imola in order to win his patronage. Maps were extremely rare at the time and it would have seemed like a new concept. Upon seeing it, Cesare hired Leonardo as his chief military engineer and architect."

What a hell of a work for a job application! It looked so accurate I couldn't resist and I started comparing the map with an aereal image and tried explaining to my son the changes of the landscape over the centuries. This is the polished version of that improvised lecture.
